About The Organization

The Ramakrishna Vivekananda Mission Federation is an organization dedicated to offering quality education and medical facilities at subsidized rates to the deprived and distressed sections of the society. This organization was founded by Swami Bhavishyananda Maharaj in the year 1969 in the city Bankura, West Bengal.

Swami Bhavishyananda Maharaj was named Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay by his parents and was immensely inclined towards spiritualism. Since childhood, the teachings of Ramakrishna paramahamsa captivated his mind. He initiated his spiritual journey through the words of wisdom said by Ramakrishna paramahamsa. Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay joined the Indian army in the early 1913s. In 1914 when the first world war broke out, India was a Princely state controlled by the United kingdom. Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay was one amongst the millions of Indian soldiers who fought with distinction throughout the world. He was part of the Indian army in the German east Africa and the western front. Amidst the anger, rage, fear, anxiety, grief, guilt and sorrows during the war, Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay constantly fell back on his spiritual learnings to maintain his sanity. Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay was immensely patriotic and revolutionary. He was highly inspired by the poetic work and music of Kazi Narzul Islam. Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay was awarded Cornel 2 gold medals for his fearlessness and mindful war strategies during the first world war. .

He cycled from Seetla to Jaya Ram Bati in the search of infinite space of wisdom, peace, silence, love, existence, consciousness and compassion. In Seetla, Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay met holy mother Sharoda Devi. He attained deekshya from the holy mother Sharoda Devi in the year 1916 at the age of 21. He offered Haritaki (Tropical Almonds) and the gold coin to her after the auspicious ceremony. Deekshya is giving of a mantra or an initiation by the guru, which marks the preparation or consecration for a religious ceremony. It is given in a one-to-one ceremony, and typically includes the taking on of a serious spiritual discipline.

Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay joined the Ramakrishna Mission, Belur in 1918. At the age of 25 ,Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay attained bharhmacharya deekshya from the then secretary (Swami Saradananda Maharaj) of the Ramakrishna Mission, Belur. Phanibhushan Mukhopadhyay took sanyaas (renunciation) from the worldly pleasures under the spiritual guidance of Swami Brahmananda Maharaj(President- Ramkrishna Mission, Belur) in the year 1921 and came to be known as Swami Bhavishyananda Maharaj. He then founded Ramakrishna Mission in rajkot, Gujarat in the year 1927. Swami Bhavishyananda Maharaj founded the Ramakrsihna Ashram, Seetla, West Bengal in the year 1950. Ramakrishna Sarva Dharma Sangh welfare society was founded and run by Swami Bhavishyananda Maharaj between 1950-1953. This welfare society ran the night school for the financially weaker sections of the society, offered free medical facilities to women and children and also catered to nutritional requirements of the growing kids and infants in the village. In the year 1953, he established a Co-Ed Bengali medium school in Seetla and also enhanced the road connectivity to Seetla from Jambeda. In 1954, the school gets affiliated to state board of West Bengal. In 1966, he constructed a well in Tapovan which was used for both domestic and irrigation purposes. In 1968,the school gets permission for High school establishment by the state board of West Bengal.1969- In 1973,Devdas Mukherjee, who is currently known as Krishnanda Maharaj met Swami Bhabishyananda.
